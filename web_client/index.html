How it works

prv.ec stores the urls you enter here in encrypted ciphertext. both encryption and decryption is done right here, in your browser. even if we wanted to, we could not tell where your link goes to, or if it's even a url for that matter. this is in contrast to all other popular url shortening services which collect all kinds of data about the visitors to their links. we don't log anything about users who visit links (you can check—the server software is free and open source software), not that it matters much since we have no way of knowing where your links go to.

the shortened url itself contains the password to decrypt the url, so anyone you share the link with who clicks on it will be redirected to the intended url. anything in the url after the "#" character is not visible to our servers and remains entirely client-side. in your shortened url, some of the characters after the "#" represent your password. several techniques are used to keep this password short while still preserving some level of secrecy. this includes performing an expensive calculation which may take several seconds and heat up your phone before the original url can be successfully decrypted and redirected for you. try it out!
