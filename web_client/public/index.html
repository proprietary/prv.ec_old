prv.ec – the private URL shortener

the world's first end-to-end encrypted URL shortener

How it works

prv.ec stores the urls you enter here in encrypted form. Both encryption and decryption is done right here, in your browser. Even if we wanted to, we could not tell where your link goes to, or if it's even a url for that matter. This is in contrast to all other popular url shortening services which collect all kinds of data about the visitors to their links. We don't log anything about users who visit links (you can check—the server software is free and open source software), not that it matters much since, once again, we have no way of knowing where your links go to.

The shortened url itself contains the password to recover the original URL, so anyone you share the link with who clicks on it will be redirected to the intended url. Anything in the url after the "#" character is not visible to our servers and remains entirely client-side. This is the password. But how can it be so short? Aren't short passwords insecure? Well this short password is used to derive a much longer password, and this process takes several seconds (and heats up your device), making brute force attacks extremely expensive even on the best hardware.
